Disease: Bacterial Leaf Streak

Common Name:

Rice bacterial panicle blight; also known as bacterial grain rot or panicle blight.

Scientific Name:

Burkholderia glumae

Distribution

Bacterial panicle blight occurs in Japan, Taiwan, Latin America and the USA.Bacterial panicle blight is not present in Australia.

Hosts

Rice (Oryza sativa) is the only known host of bacterial panicle blight.

Symptoms

  • This is characterized as having upright, straw-colored panicles containing florets with a draker base and reddish-brown line (margin of lesion) across the floret between the darker area and the straw-colored area, resulting in abortion of the kernel before it fills
  • It can be seen on plantlets, on the sheath of the flag leaves, and on panicles.
  • The spikelet that has been infected has a straw-coloring and there is also discoloration of the grain as well as rotting and panicle blanking.
  • When conditions are ideal the bacteria quickly multiply.
  • Symptoms start to appear 3 days after inoculating the panicles with a gradual increase in the number of panicles affected.

Identification of pathogen

  • This disease can cause yield losses of up to 80% when environmental conditions are in favor of the bacteria’s development, i.e. temperatures around 32-37ºC and relative humidity above 80%.
Factors favouring disease development

  • The frequent use of susceptible seeds.
  • Monoculture.
  • Moving the seed from one area to another.
  • Poor agrochemical management practices.
  • Highly dense crops.
  • Inopportune planting times

Management Strategies

  • Oxolinic acid (Straner) is the only effective chemical control available for this pathogen when used as a spary.
  • Use of pathogen-free seeds is an important practice to reduce or manage the incidence of bacterial panicle blight.
  • Seed treatment
  • Treatemt of seed in presprouting water
  • Heat treatment of seed
  • disease resistance
  • Copper Fungicides
